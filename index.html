Theory of Pipe Friction Losses

Friction losses occur in pipes due to the resistance offered by the pipe walls to the flow of fluid. This resistance results in a reduction of pressure and energy along the length of the pipe. The major factors influencing pipe friction losses include:

  • Pipe length (L): Longer pipes tend to have higher friction losses.
  • Fluid velocity (v): Higher velocities lead to increased friction losses.
  • Pipe diameter (D): Smaller diameters result in higher friction losses.
  • Fluid viscosity (μ): Viscous fluids experience higher friction losses.
  • Roughness of the pipe surface: Rough surfaces increase friction losses.

Understanding and quantifying these losses are crucial for designing efficient piping systems and predicting flow behavior.
